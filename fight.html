FIGHT

In Kentucky, one of the gardener's biggest foes is humidity, especially in the Ohio Valley. Humidity is a breeding ground for fungal diseases like powdery mildew, blight, and anthracnose. The constant water content in the air during the summer makes these diseases spread even if it doesn't rain. The worst insects in the area include beetles, vine borers, squash bugs, and tomato hornworms. Many of the most harmful insects and diseases begin showing up in June when the weather starts to get significantly warmer. Read on to learn more about some of Kentucky's worst offenders!

EAT

It takes a lot of time and work to grow a garden, but the rewards are always plentiful in the end... food! Whether you're growing vegetables, fruit, herbs or all of the above, you're going to have a lot of food that needs to be eaten. Salads, soups, stir-frys, the only limit is your imagination. Mix and match different vegetables to create a flavor combination that fits your individual taste. Use your herbs to give additional spice to any meal! This page contains some recipe ideas that will help you turn those raw vegetables into delicious side dishes and meals that the whole family can enjoy.

QUICK & EASY RECIPES

Oven Roasted Salsa

INGREDIENTS


• 2 pounds of tomatoes, halved
• 6 unpeeled garlic cloves
• 1 large white onion, peeled and cut into ½-inch rounds
• 1 large jalapeño, cored and halved
• 1 tablespoon freshly-squeezed lime juice
• 1 teaspoon fine sea salt


INSTRUCTIONS


• Preheat broiler to high.
• Arrange the tomatoes (skin side up), garlic cloves, onion and jalapeño (skin side up) in an even layer on a large baking sheet. Broil for 6-8 minutes, or until the tomatoes and jalapeños have blistered and blackened pretty thoroughly on top.
• Remove pan from the oven, and peel and discard the skin from the garlic cloves. Transfer the tomatoes, garlic, onion and jalapeño to a blender. Add in the remaining ingredients and puree the salsa until smooth.
• Taste, and season with extra salt or lime juice if needed.

Asian Cucumber Salad

INGREDIENTS


• 2 cucumbers - halved lengthwise, seeded, sliced
• 2 chopped green onions
• 2 teaspoons salt
• 1/2 cup rice vinegar
• 1/4 cup white sugar
• 2 tablespoons sesame oil
• 1 tablespoon minced garlic
• 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger root
• 1 tablespoon sesame seeds


INSTRUCTIONS


• Once the cucumbers are chopped, add them to a single large bowl and sprinkle with salt. Let stand for 20 minutes and drain out any excess water.
• Add the rest of the ingredients and toss it all together.
• Put the mixture in the refrigerator for a few hours. If you can leave it overnight, that's even better.
• Serve once the cucumbers have shrunken somewhat and absorbed the other liquids.

Garlic Scape Hummus

INGREDIENTS


• 1 fifteen ounce can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
• 1/2 cup tahini sauce
• 1/2 cup thinly sliced garlic scapes, chopped
• 4 tablespoons lemon juice
• 6 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
• 3/4 teaspoon fine sea salt
• 1/2 teaspoon sweet Hungarian paprika


INSTRUCTIONS


• Combine all ingredients in a blender with the liquids on the bottom and blend until completely smooth.
• If you like, you can dress it up for a party by swirling it with the back of a spoon and garnishing with some olive oil, flaky sea salt, paprika, and thinly sliced scapes or other herbs.

Herb-Roasted Beer Can Chicken

INGREDIENTS


• 1 fresh whole young chicken
• 1 can of beer
• 1 tablespoon salt
• 1 tablespoon pepper
• 1 tablespoon lemon pepper
• 2 tablespoons brown sugar
• 1/2 tablespoon paprika
• 1/2 tablespoon garlic powder
• 1/2 tablespoon dried basil


INSTRUCTIONS


• Remove any giblets and wash the chicken.
• Make the rub using the spices listed above and apply to the skin and inside the cavity.
• Insert a half full can of beer into the cavity and stand the chicken over top of it.
• Heat the grill to 350°F and use indirect cooking (meaning don't place the chicken directly over the flame).
• Rotate the chicken a quarter turn every 15 minutes.
• After one hour, check the temperature in the thigh area. 165-170° is ready to be eaten.

Basil Pesto

INGREDIENTS


• 1 quart scallions, blanched
• 1 bunch parsley, blanched
• 2 cups basil, blanched
• 1/3 cup marcona almonds, diced
• 1/3 cup lemon juice
• 1/2 cup extra virgin olive oil
• 1 cup parmesan cheese, grated


INSTRUCTIONS


• Blanch the scallions, parsley, and basil in boiling water for about 30 seconds and then place them in ice water for about a minute.
• Remove them and absorb as much water from the greens as possible using paper towels.
• Place the greens and the rest of the ingredients in a blender and blend until smooth.
• Finish with olive oil and season to taste.

Habañero & Garlic Hot Sauce

INGREDIENTS


• 8 habañero peppers stemmed and halved
• 1 cup water
• 4 cups white vinegar
• 10 garlic cloves peeled and coarsely chopped
• 1 onion, chopped
• 1 tablespoon sugar
• 3 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
• 1/2 cup lime juice
• 1 teaspoon Hungarian paprika
• 1 teaspoon salt


INSTRUCTIONS


• Preheat oven to 425°F.
• Place the peppers on a baking sheet and roast in the oven for 10 minutes or until the skins begin to blister.
• Heat a large pot to medium heat and add the remaining ingredients. Bring to a boil then reduce heat to low and simmer about 20 minutes, or until the onions and garlic soften.
• Add roasted peppers and simmer for about 5 minutes.
• Transfer the mixture to a food processor and puree until smooth.
• Strain the sauce into sterilized bottles and let it cool until ready to use.

Vegetable Soup

INGREDIENTS


• 2 tablespoons olive oil
• 1.5 cups onion, chopped
• 2 cups carrots, peeled and chopped
• 2 cups mushrooms
• 1 cup frozen or fresh corn
• 1 cup frozen or fresh peas
• 4 cloves garlic, minced
• 4 cups beef, chicken or vegetable broth
• 2 cups diced tomatoes
• 3 cups potatoes, peeled and diced
• 1/3 cup fresh parsley, chopped
• 2 bay leaves
• 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
• Salt and freshly ground black pepper


INSTRUCTIONS


• Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
• Add onions and carrots and sauté 4 minutes, then add garlic and sauté for another minute.
• Add in broth, tomatoes, potatoes, parsley, bay leaves, and thyme. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
• Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low, cover and simmer until potatoes are almost fully tender, usually about 20-30 minutes.
• Add corn, mushrooms and peas and cook 5 minutes more.
• Remove the bay leaves and let the soup stand for about 10 minutes before serving.

Tomato Soup

INGREDIENTS


• 2 pounds tomato meat
• 4 tablespoons butter
• 1 onion, chopped
• 2 cloves garlic, chopped
• 2 cups chicken stock
• Tomato water (amount can vary)


INSTRUCTIONS


• Boil tomatoes for 30 seconds, place them in an ice bath to loosen the skins, then peel.
• Brown the onions and the garlic in the butter and let them soften.
• Add chicken stock and pour in any tomato water left after peeling.
• When the mixture begins to simmer, add the tomatoes.
• Cook for 40 minutes on a stovetop on medium heat, stirring occasionally.
• Put the mixture in a blender and puree until smooth.
• Just as good cold as it is hot!

Butternut Squash Soup

INGREDIENTS


• 4 pounds whole butternut squash, halved lengthwise and seeds removed
• 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
• 1 medium Granny Smith apple
• 1/2 medium yellow onion
• 8 fresh sage leaves
• 2.5 cups vegetable or chicken broth
• 2.5 cups water
• 1.5 teaspoons kosher salt
• 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
• 1/3 cup heavy cream


INSTRUCTIONS


• Heat the oven to 425°F.
• Melt 1 tablespoon of butter and brush it over the tops and insides of the squash halves.
• Season with salt and pepper and roast until tender, about 50 minutes to 1 hour.
• Meanwhile, peel, core, and cut the apples and onions.
• Melt the remaining tablespoon of butter in a large saucepan over medium heat.
• Add the apple, onion, and sage and cook them, stirring occasionally, until softened. Remove the pan from the heat and set it aside.
• When the squash is ready, scoop the flesh into the saucepan with the sautéed apples and onions; discard the skins.
• Add the broth, water, and stir to combine. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to low and simmer, stirring occasionally and breaking up any large pieces of squash, until the flavors meld, about 15 minutes. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the cream.
• Using a blender, purée the soup in batches until smooth.

Pumpkin Chocolate Chip Cookies

INGREDIENTS


• 1 cup puréed pumpkin
• 1 cup white sugar
• 1/2 cup vegetable oil
• 1 egg
• 2 cups all-purpose flour
• 2 teaspoons baking powder
• 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
• 1/2 teaspoon salt
• 1 teaspoon baking soda
• 1 teaspoon milk
• 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
• 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips


INSTRUCTIONS


• Combine pumpkin, sugar, vegetable oil, and egg.
• In a separate bowl, stir together flour, baking powder, ground cinnamon, and salt.
• Dissolve the baking soda with the milk and stir.
• Add flour mixture to pumpkin mixture and mix well.
• Add vanilla and chocolate chips.
• Drop by the spoonful on a greased cookie sheet and bake at 350°F for approximately 10 minutes, or until lightly brown and firm.

GROW

Growing a garden in Kentucky can be a little challenging when compared to other parts of the United States that stay warmer all year round. Kentucky is in Hardiness Zone 6, an area known for mild climates with cold winters and humid summers. The state's growing season lasts from early spring to early fall, with the winter's last frost occurring in mid-March and the first frost falling in November.
